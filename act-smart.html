Practical Actions & Impact

A practical solution library for Yangon: actions people can start at home, in school, and during daily travel without pretending every solution fits every budget.

Awareness Video

See The Actions In Real Life

A Wattwise awareness video turn simple energy habits into everyday action.

Home

Daylight Before Lighting

Open curtains, move study desks near windows, and turn on only the lights needed for the task.

  • Check rooms before sunset.
  • Use task lamps instead of whole-room lighting.
Home

Fan-First Cooling

Use shade, airflow, and fans before stronger cooling. Keep doors closed when cooling one room.

  • Clean fan blades monthly.
  • Cool only occupied rooms.
Home

Standby Power Basket

Group chargers, speakers, and small devices on one power strip so they are easy to switch off.

  • Unplug after charging.
  • Turn off TV boxes at night.
Home

Fridge Door Discipline

Decide what you need before opening the fridge and keep warm food outside until it cools.

  • Do quick door checks.
  • Leave space behind the fridge.
Home

Water Pump Schedule

Set a household pump time and turn it off immediately after tanks are filled.

  • Fix leaking taps early.
  • Avoid repeated short pump runs.
Home

Batch Heat Tasks

Iron clothes in groups and boil only the water needed for tea, noodles, or coffee.

  • Do one ironing session.
  • Use lids while heating.
School

End-of-Class Checklist

Make one student team check fans, lights, projectors, speakers, and extension cords after each class.

  • Rotate the team weekly.
  • Post the checklist near the door.
School

Daylight Seating Map

Arrange seats so students can use natural light without blocking walkways or causing glare.

  • Keep windows clear.
  • Use fewer lights in bright periods.
School

Computer Room Shutdown

Turn off monitors, CPUs, speakers, and stabilizers when the lab is not in use.

  • Use a shutdown captain.
  • Check before lunch and closing time.
School

Projector Time Plan

Switch projectors on only when the lesson needs them and turn them off during discussion time.

  • Prepare slides before class.
  • Use board work when possible.
School

Weekly Energy Audit

Students record where energy is wasted and suggest one improvement for the next week.

  • Track repeated problems.
  • Share results in assembly.
School

Energy Reminder Posters

Place short reminders near switches, classroom doors, and computer tables.

  • Use student-made posters.
  • Change messages monthly.
Daily Life

Cook Once, Reheat Less

Plan meals so rice, curry, and hot water are prepared in fewer heating sessions.

  • Use lids while cooking.
  • Turn warmers off after meals.
Daily Life

Charge With A Plan

Charge phones and power banks together, then unplug chargers instead of leaving them in sockets.

  • Use one charging station.
  • Remove full devices quickly.
Daily Life

Sun-Dry When Possible

Use sunlight and airflow for clothes instead of extra electric drying or repeated fan use.

  • Wash full loads.
  • Spin-dry before hanging.
Daily Life

Screen Brightness Habit

Lower phone and laptop brightness indoors and use sleep mode during breaks.

  • Set auto-sleep timers.
  • Close unused apps.
Daily Life

Repair Before Replacing

Fix loose plugs, broken switches, and damaged wires early so appliances do not waste power or become unsafe.

  • Ask an adult or technician.
  • Do not use damaged cords.
Daily Life

Buy With Efficiency In Mind

When buying appliances, compare size, energy label, repair support, and real household need.

  • Avoid oversized appliances.
  • Choose durable products.
Transportation

Plan Trips Before Leaving

Group nearby errands, avoid unnecessary repeat trips, and choose routes that reduce waiting and idling.

  • List stops before leaving.
  • Avoid repeat trips on the same route.
Transportation

Shared Ride Routine

Share rides for school or work when it is safe, planned, and practical for everyone.

  • Set fixed pickup times.
  • Avoid half-empty repeat trips.
Transportation

Walk Short Distances

For nearby shops, tuition, or friends' houses, walk when the route, daylight, and weather are safe.

  • Choose shaded routes.
  • Travel with friends when needed.
Transportation

Reduce Engine Idling

When waiting safely for a longer time, turn off the engine instead of burning fuel while parked.

  • Plan waiting points.
  • Avoid blocking traffic.
Transportation

Cycle Where It Fits

Use bicycles for short, safe routes and keep tires, brakes, and lights in good condition.

  • Pick low-traffic streets.
  • Carry rain protection.
Transportation

Route Check Habit

Check traffic and weather before leaving so trips use less time, fuel, and frustration.

  • Leave before rush where possible.
  • Combine pickup and shopping stops.
Smart Tech

Smart Plug Control

Use smart plugs or switched power strips to schedule chargers, routers, and TV devices off when not needed.

  • Start with one high-use corner.
  • Check schedules every week.
Smart Tech

Timer Switch Routine

Use timer switches for lights, fans, signboards, and classroom devices that are often forgotten.

  • Set shutdown times clearly.
  • Override only when needed.
Smart Tech

Energy Meter Check

Use a plug-in meter or regular meter photo log to see which habits change electricity use.

  • Measure one appliance at a time.
  • Compare weekly readings.
Smart Tech

Motion Sensor Lighting

Use motion sensors in corridors, stairways, toilets, or storage rooms where lights are often left on.

  • Use sensors in low-traffic areas.
  • Keep manual switches available.
Smart Tech

Inverter Appliance Upgrade

When replacing fans, refrigerators, or air conditioners, compare inverter models and real room size.

  • Replace only when needed.
  • Avoid oversized appliances.
Smart Tech

Smart Temperature Rule

Set clear temperature rules for cooling rooms and use fans, curtains, and timers together.

  • Cool only occupied rooms.
  • Use timers during study sessions.
Renewables

Solar Study Lamp

Use solar lamps for study tables, reading corners, and emergency lighting when sunlight is available.

  • Charge near sunlight safely.
  • Keep one lamp for study time.
Renewables

Solar Charging Station

Create a small solar charging point for phones, study lights, or power banks where it is practical.

  • Charge during strong daylight.
  • Protect batteries from heat.
Renewables

Solar Path Lighting

Use solar lights for gates, footpaths, school walkways, or outdoor community spaces.

  • Place panels where shade is low.
  • Clean panels regularly.
Renewables

Rooftop Solar Readiness

For schools or businesses, check roof strength, sunlight, budget, and maintenance before rooftop solar.

  • Ask a qualified installer.
  • Plan battery safety first.
Renewables

Wind Energy Site Check

Wind energy can help in windy places, but Yangon users should compare wind strength, safety, and cost first.

  • Use it as a comparison study.
  • Check space and safety rules.
Renewables

Micro-Hydro Learning

Study how flowing water can create electricity in suitable places, then compare it with Yangon's urban limits.

  • Use it for research projects.
  • Compare site and safety needs.
