Myanmar and the World: Energy Crossroads

Crossroads compares Myanmar's electricity consumption with practical energy-saving habits from around the world. The goal is to choose lessons Yangon homes, schools, shops, and travelers can adapt.

Why Compare Globally?

Global examples show how public awareness, efficient appliances, transport choices, renewable energy, and long-term planning influence energy behaviour.

For Yangon, the lesson is simple:

  • Reduce unnecessary electricity waste
  • Choose energy-efficient devices
  • Build smarter daily habits
  • Adapt solutions to local electricity challenges

The goal is smarter energy use, not only lower energy consumption.

AVOIDABLE ENERGY WASTE

Standby Power Loss

5-10%

of household electricity use can be consumed by devices left plugged in or on standby, such as chargers, televisions, routers, speakers, and appliances. Unplugging unused devices is a realistic habit that can reduce avoidable waste in Yangon homes.

Yangon Consumption Pressure

Growing daily electricity needs make habits important in Yangon homes, schools, shops, and transport. Better habits cannot solve the energy system alone, but they reduce avoidable demand during crowded hours.
