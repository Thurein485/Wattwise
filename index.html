Purpose of Our Website

Wattwise connects real energy data, global comparison, and practical action guides so Yangon students, households, shops, and communities can reduce waste and build smarter habits.

Understand Local Data

  • Use real electricity consumption data instead of only access numbers.

Compare Global Practices

  • Turn global examples into realistic local choices, not copy-paste solutions.

Act With Practical Habits

  • Choose home, school, daily-life, and transport actions that can start now.
  • Build habits that support a cleaner and more resilient Yangon.

Contextual Note: Myanmar

Myanmar’s lower electricity use is not automatically a sign of strong efficiency. Wattwise uses these 2025 planning estimates as context, then connects them to daily habits such as lighting, charging devices, cooking, cooling, and transport.

380-390 kWh per person, 2025 estimate

Electricity use per person remains low compared to many countries in Southeast Asia, influenced by limited access, blackouts, and increasing use of alternative energy sources such as solar.

20,500-21,000 GWh national consumption, 2025 estimate

Myanmar's total electricity consumption is expected to remain around this range because of population needs, urban growth, and uneven electricity supply.

The Yangon Consumption Gap

Myanmar's electricity consumption data gives Wattwise a realistic background for Yangon in 2025 and beyond. The goal is to analyse usage patterns, compare practical solutions, and reduce avoidable energy waste in homes, schools, shops, and daily travel. As Yangon develops, future energy efficiency depends not only on consuming less electricity, but on using energy more intelligently through efficient appliances, reduced waste, renewable energy adoption, and smarter daily habits.
